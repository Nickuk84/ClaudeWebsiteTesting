UK Tax Strategy

Our approach to tax management and compliance in the United Kingdom.

Introduction

This tax strategy statement is published in accordance with the requirements of paragraph 16(2) of Schedule 19 of the Finance Act 2016 and applies to the UK activities of AEGIS London. It sets out our approach to tax risk management, tax planning, the level of acceptable tax risk, and our relationship with HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC). This strategy is reviewed and approved by the Board annually.

Our Approach to Tax Risk Management

AEGIS London is committed to maintaining a robust tax governance framework. We seek to comply fully with all applicable tax laws and regulations in the UK and in every jurisdiction in which we operate. Our tax affairs are managed by appropriately qualified professionals, and we maintain effective internal controls and processes to ensure the accuracy and timeliness of our tax filings and payments.

We take a conservative approach to tax risk and do not engage in aggressive tax planning arrangements. Where tax law is unclear or subject to interpretation, we seek professional external advice and, where appropriate, engage proactively with HMRC to obtain certainty over our tax position.

Tax Planning

Our approach to tax planning is aligned with our commercial and economic activities. We seek to use available tax reliefs and incentives as intended by the UK government, and we do not enter into artificial arrangements whose sole purpose is to reduce our tax liability. All tax planning activities are undertaken with regard to their reputational impact and alignment with both the spirit and the letter of the law.

Level of Acceptable Risk

AEGIS London has a low tolerance for tax risk. We aim to ensure that our tax affairs are managed in a manner that provides certainty and minimises the risk of unexpected tax liabilities, penalties, or reputational damage. Significant or complex tax matters are escalated to senior management and, where appropriate, to the Board or Audit Committee for consideration and oversight.

Relationship with HMRC

We are committed to maintaining an open, transparent, and constructive relationship with HMRC. We engage proactively on matters of uncertainty, respond to enquiries in a timely and cooperative manner, and disclose all relevant facts and circumstances when submitting tax returns and making tax-related claims. We seek to resolve any disputes through open dialogue and, where possible, through real-time engagement with HMRC.
