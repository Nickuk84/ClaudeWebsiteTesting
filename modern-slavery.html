Modern Slavery Act Statement

Our commitment to preventing modern slavery and human trafficking.

Introduction

This statement is made pursuant to section 54 of the Modern Slavery Act 2015 and sets out the steps that AEGIS London has taken, and continues to take, to ensure that modern slavery and human trafficking are not taking place within our business or supply chains. AEGIS London is committed to acting ethically and with integrity in all of our business dealings and relationships, and to implementing and enforcing effective systems and controls to ensure that modern slavery is not taking place anywhere within our organisation.

Our Business

AEGIS London is a specialist insurer operating in the Lloyd's market through Syndicate 1225. We underwrite a diversified portfolio of property, casualty, and specialty insurance and reinsurance business. Our operations are primarily based in London with additional presence in Toronto, Canada. As a professional services organisation in the financial sector, our supply chain principally consists of professional services providers, technology vendors, and facilities management companies.

Our Policies and Due Diligence

We maintain a number of policies and procedures that are relevant to countering modern slavery, including our Code of Conduct, Whistleblowing Policy, Procurement Policy, and Supplier Due Diligence processes. All employees are expected to adhere to these policies and to report any concerns or suspicions of modern slavery through our confidential reporting channels.

Our due diligence processes in relation to modern slavery include evaluating new suppliers against modern slavery criteria, reviewing existing suppliers on an ongoing basis, conducting risk assessments of our supply chain, and including anti-slavery provisions in our contracts with suppliers. We are committed to ensuring that our suppliers and business partners share our commitment to the prevention of modern slavery.

Risk Assessment and Management

We recognise that as a financial services company based in the UK, our direct exposure to modern slavery risk is relatively low. However, we remain vigilant and take proactive steps to identify and mitigate any potential risks within our supply chains. We assess our supply chain on an ongoing basis and prioritise areas where the risk of modern slavery may be higher, taking appropriate action where necessary.

Training and Awareness

We are committed to raising awareness of modern slavery issues among our employees. Relevant staff receive training on how to identify and respond to potential indicators of modern slavery, and we ensure that our procurement teams understand the requirements of the Modern Slavery Act and our own policies and procedures.

Reporting and Governance

This statement has been approved by the Board of Directors of AEGIS London and will be reviewed and updated annually. We encourage anyone who has concerns about modern slavery in any part of our business or supply chain to raise them through our confidential reporting channels.
